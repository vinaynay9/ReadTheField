HOW TO INTERPRET OUTPUTS

Monte Carlo simulations replay the matchup thousands of times using historical context and uncertainty to produce a distribution of outcomes. These are probabilistic ranges, not single-point predictions or guarantees.

P10 is the downside (floor), P50 is the median (most likely outcome), and P90 is the upside (ceiling). Use the tables for exact percentile values and the charts to see the full shape of the distribution.

Wide distributions imply volatility, narrow distributions imply stability. For RB/WR/TE, touchdowns are reported only as total_touchdowns.
